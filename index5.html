Basil Ocimum basilicum Basil can be cultivated in gardens or grown in pots as a useful culinary herb. It grows to a maximum hieght of 2 feet, with broad leaves and spikey creamy/white flowers. Basil is now cultivated in many leaf variteies that range from white to purple. There is lemon scented basil, a purple basil lettuce, and a bush basil. "Basil is a useful culinary herb, widely used in Mediterranean dishes, and especially complementary to tomatoes and garlic. The fresh leavs should only be added towards the end of the cooking to preserve the flavor. Medical herbalists use basil as an antispasmodic, to aid digestion, settle the stomach, and relieve nausea. /td> Basil contins an essential oil - estragol - which is used in insect repellent manufacture.
